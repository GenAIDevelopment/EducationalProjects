## Acme Corp - Employee Handbook

---

### **Company Email Usage Policy**

**Policy Brief and Purpose**

The Company Email Usage Policy at Acme Corp is designed to guide employees on the appropriate and responsible use of their corporate email addresses. Recognizing that email is a critical tool for daily operations, this policy aims to clarify the limitations and expectations associated with its use, thereby safeguarding the company's confidential data, protecting its reputation, and ensuring the security of its technological assets.

**Scope**

This email usage policy applies to all employees, contractors, and any other individuals who are assigned or granted access to Acme Corp's email system. This includes individually assigned email addresses as well as departmental or shared accounts.

**Policy Components**

Acme Corp provides corporate email as a vital communication tool to facilitate business operations. While the primary purpose is for company-related correspondence, employees are permitted reasonable personal use, provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities or violate any company policies.

**Correct Use of Corporate Email**

Employees of Acme Corp are permitted to use their company email for all work-related purposes without limitation. This includes, but is not limited to:

*   Communicating with current and prospective clients, partners, and vendors.
*   Accessing necessary software and online services required for job functions.
*   Sharing contact information at professional events, conferences, or career fairs for business development.
*   Subscribing to industry-related newsletters, online services, or platforms that contribute to professional growth and job performance.

**Inappropriate Use of Corporate Email**

As corporate email users represent Acme Corp, they must refrain from the following actions:

*   Sending insulting, discriminatory, or offensive content.
*   Registering for competitive services or products without explicit authorization.
*   Intentionally sending unsolicited bulk emails (spam), especially to colleagues.
*   Signing up for unreliable, illegal, suspect, or disreputable websites or services.
*   Sending emails containing unlawful marketing information or solicitations.

Acme Corp reserves the right to monitor corporate email usage to ensure compliance with this policy and to protect company interests.

**Personal Use**

Employees may use their corporate email for limited personal matters, provided these activities do not:

*   Interfere with work responsibilities or productivity.
*   Violate any company policy, including those related to confidentiality, harassment, or data security.
*   Involve the transmission of inappropriate or offensive content.
*   Consume excessive company resources.

Examples of acceptable personal use include:

*   Registering for professional development classes or community meetups that may be beneficial.
*   Sending occasional, non-business-related emails to friends and family, provided no confidential information is disclosed and the emails do not constitute spam.
*   Downloading safe and appropriate personal use content, such as guides or ebooks, provided it does not pose a security risk.

**Email Security**

Email is a common vector for malware, confidentiality breaches, and cyberattacks. Employees must adhere to the following security practices:

*   **Strong Passwords:** Set strong, unique passwords for email accounts. Passwords must have a minimum of eight characters, including a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using personal information (e.g., birthdays, names) or easily guessable sequences.
*   **Password Management:** Change email passwords every four to five months. Do not write down passwords in easily accessible locations.
*   **Attachment and Link Caution:** Exercise extreme caution when opening attachments or clicking on links in emails from unknown or suspicious senders. If the content is inadequately explained or seems unusual, do not engage.
*   **Sender Verification:** Always verify the sender's identity and email address to ensure legitimacy. Look for inconsistencies in sender names or email domains.
*   **Style Red Flags:** Be wary of emails containing grammatical errors, excessive use of exclamation marks, or all-capital letters, as these can be indicators of phishing attempts.
*   **Reporting Suspicious Emails:** If an employee is uncertain about the safety of an email, they should contact the Acme Corp IT Security department immediately for assistance.
*   **Anti-Malware Updates:** Ensure that anti-malware and antivirus software on company devices are kept up-to-date.

**Email Signature**

Acme Corp encourages employees to create professional email signatures that accurately represent the company. Signatures should include:

*   Employee Name
*   Employee Title
*   Company Name (with a link to the company website)
*   Company Phone Number
*   Company Address

Optional elements may include company logos or relevant work-related links, subject to IT department approval. Employees unsure about creating an appropriate signature should consult their direct supervisor or the IT department.

**Disciplinary Consequences**

Employees who fail to adhere to this email usage policy will be subject to disciplinary action, which may include, but is not limited to, verbal warnings, written warnings, suspension, or termination of employment. Examples of policy violations that may lead to disciplinary action include:

*   Using company email to send unauthorized confidential information.
*   Sending inappropriate, offensive, or harassing emails to colleagues, clients, or partners.
*   Using the corporate email address for any unlawful activity.
