## Acme Corp - Employee Handbook

### **Company Email Usage Policy**

**Policy Brief and Purpose**

The Acme Corp corporate email system is a vital tool for business communication. This policy outlines the acceptable and unacceptable uses of company email to ensure data security, protect company reputation, and maintain a productive work environment. It aims to safeguard confidential information and technological assets.

**Scope**

This policy applies to all Acme Corp employees, contractors, and any third parties who are assigned or granted access to a company email account. This includes individually assigned accounts and departmental email addresses.

**Policy Components**

Acme Corp provides company email accounts to facilitate efficient business operations. While the primary use is for work-related communication, limited personal use is permitted under specific conditions.

**Correct Use of Corporate Email**

Employees are permitted to use their company email for all work-related purposes. This includes, but is not limited to:

*   Communicating with current and prospective clients, partners, and vendors.
*   Accessing necessary software and online services for job functions.
*   Sharing contact information at professional events for business purposes.
*   Subscribing to industry-related newsletters or online services that enhance job performance or professional growth.

**Inappropriate Use of Corporate Email**

As company email users represent Acme Corp, the following uses are strictly prohibited:

*   Sending insulting, discriminatory, or offensive content.
*   Registering for competitive services or products without explicit authorization.
*   Intentionally sending unsolicited bulk emails (spam), especially to colleagues.
*   Signing up for unreliable, illegal, suspect, or disreputable websites or services.
*   Sending emails containing unlawful marketing information or solicitations.
*   Using company email for personal financial gain or any illegal activity.

Acme Corp reserves the right to monitor all company email communications.

**Personal Use**

Limited personal use of company email is allowed, provided it does not interfere with job responsibilities or violate any other company policies. Permitted personal use includes:

*   Registering for professional development classes or meetups.
*   Sending occasional, brief emails to friends and family, provided no confidential information is disclosed and it does not constitute spam.
*   Downloading safe and appropriate personal content, such as guides or ebooks, that do not pose a security risk.

**Email Security**

Email is a common vector for malware, phishing attacks, and data breaches. Employees must adhere to the following security practices:

*   **Password Strength:** Set strong passwords with a minimum of eight characters, including a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using personal information (e.g., birthdays) or easily guessable patterns.
*   **Password Management:** Change email passwords every four to six months. Do not write down passwords in easily accessible locations.
*   **Phishing and Malware Awareness:** Be vigilant about suspicious emails. Do not open attachments or click on links from unknown or untrusted senders, especially if the content is unexpected or the subject line is misleading. Verify sender identities and look for grammatical errors or inconsistencies.
*   **Reporting Suspicious Emails:** If an employee is unsure about the safety of an email, they should contact the Acme Corp IT Security department immediately.
*   **Anti-Malware:** Ensure anti-malware software on company devices is kept updated.

**Email Signature**

Employees are encouraged to use a professional email signature that accurately represents Acme Corp. A standard signature format should include:

*   Employee Name
*   Employee Title
*   Acme Corp (with a link to the company website)
*   Company Phone Number
*   Company Address

Company logos, work-related images, and approved links may be included. Employees can seek assistance from their direct supervisor or the IT department for signature setup.

**Disciplinary Consequences**

Employees who fail to comply with this email usage policy will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Examples of violations include:

*   Unauthorized transmission of confidential company information.
*   Sending inappropriate, offensive, or harassing emails to colleagues, clients, or partners.
*   Using company email for any unlawful activity.
